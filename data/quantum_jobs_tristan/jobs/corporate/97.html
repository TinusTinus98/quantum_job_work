Quantum Research Scientist – Device Fabrication, Quantum Computing

Job ID: 2034477 | Amazon Dev Center U.S., Inc.

Apply now

DESCRIPTION

Job summary
The Amazon Web Services (AWS) Center for Quantum Computing in Pasadena, CA, is looking to hire a Research Scientist with experience in semiconductor process development who will aid in AWS’s effort to bring cloud quantum computing services to its customer base. Through your work inside and outside of the cleanroom environment in the device fabrication group, you will solve problems related to the fabrication of novel quantum processors.

Candidates must have a demonstrated background in sound engineering principles, and must have superlative data analysis, bias for action, problem solving, and communication skills and be highly motivated and curious to research and learn new technical topics as needed. Working effectively within a team environment is critical.

Inclusive Team Culture
Here at AWS, we embrace our differences. We are committed to furthering our culture of inclusion. We have ten employee-led affinity groups, reaching 40,000 employees in over 190 chapters globally. We have innovative benefit offerings, and host annual and ongoing learning experiences, including our Conversations on Race and Ethnicity (CORE) and AmazeCon (gender diversity) conferences.

Work/Life Balance
Our team puts a high value on work-life balance. It isn’t about how many hours you spend at home or at work; it’s about the flow you establish that brings energy to both parts of your life. We believe striking the right balance between your personal and professional life is critical to life-long happiness and fulfillment. We offer flexibility in working hours and encourage you to find your own balance between your work and personal lives.

Mentorship & Career Growth
Our team is dedicated to supporting new members. We have a broad mix of experience levels and tenures, and we’re building an environment that celebrates knowledge sharing and mentorship. Our senior members enjoy one-on-one mentoring and thorough, but kind, code reviews. We care about your career growth and strive to assign projects based on what will help each team member develop into a better-rounded engineer and enable them to take on more complex tasks in the future.


Key job responsibilities
Responsibilities include developing, improving, and sustaining fabrication and packaging process flows; collecting and analyzing inline metrology and electrical test data; writing and maintaining production SOPs; interacting with project leads to provide feedback that continuously improves the process.

A day in the life
The candidate will perform hands-on fabrication tasks using semiconductor fab equipment inside cleanroom, while developing process technology for future quantum processors. Outside of cleanroom the candidate will plan experiments, process data, and think of future innovations.
The role may include off hours work including evenings, nights and weekends.

About the team
Fabrication team within AWS Center for Quantum computing is responsible for delivering quantum computing product chips and implementing groundbreaking process innovations.

BASIC QUALIFICATIONS

· PhD Degree in one of the following or closely related fields: material science, electrical engineering, physics, chemical engineering, and mechanical engineering
· 2+ years of hands-on expertise in wafer level wet and dry etching techniques (particularly DRIE); photolithography; wet benches and wafer cleaning techniques; thin film deposition techniques (e.g. PECVD, LPCVD, ALD, evaporation, and sputtering)
· Great problem solving and analytical skills
· Strong verbal, written, and data presentation skills
· Effective at managing multiple tasks/projects and working independently or within a team environment with other engineers, scientists, and technicians who might have a wide range of technical skill levels

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS

· Experience with quantum physics and familiarity with superconducting quantum bits and circuits
. Experience in fabricating superconducting quantum circuit and/or packaging components
. Hands-on experience with electron beam lithography; lift-off; furnace oxidation; electroplating; wafer thinning; chemical mechanical polishing; wafer level and flip-chip packaging techniques; temporary and permanent wafer level bonding; 2.5/3D integration schemes; process integration
. Hands-on experience with metrology techniques such as, AFM, SEM, profilometry, reflectometry, and ellipsometry
. Experience with performing failure analysis and root cause investigation for process excursions
. Working knowledge of statistical analysis and design of experiments using JMP or minitab
. Experience with yield monitoring, statical process control, and demonstrating process capability (Ppk & Cpk)
