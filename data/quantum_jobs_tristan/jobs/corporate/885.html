Quantum-Classical Compiler Engineer 

 

About Zapata Computing

Zapata computing is a quantum software, services, and applications company developing quantum algorithms to solve critical problems across different industries, such as chemistry, material science, fintech and AI. Pioneering commercial quantum algorithms, research at Zapata is fast-paced, highly collaborative, and cross-disciplinary. ​Zapata is hiring creative, curious, and resourceful problem solvers to join our world-class team of quantum scientists, researchers, and programmers. ​ 

  

About the Role

Zapata's Quantum Hardware team is the vital link between Zapata’s engineering and research efforts and multiple quantum hardware platforms. This position will leverage your technical and communication skills to help push our scientific and engineering efforts in collaboration with our hardware partners. You will help build the toolset necessary to develop and run hybrid quantum/classical software on given hardware. The position seeks to solve problems on the new boundary between quantum and classical compiling with a desire to seamlessly merge the two realms. 

  

Key Responsibilities Include 

  • Gaining insights on various hardware platforms and serving as an internal resource on knowledge of the hardware platforms. 
  • If applicable, collaborate with external partner teams. 
  • Author scientific papers with peers and partners. 
  • Produce patent inventions on quantum and/or classical compiling toolchain

  

Required Knowledge/Skills/Abilities  

  • A Master’s in Physics, Engineering, Computer Science, or related field. 
  • At least 1 year of academic research experience or 1 year of industry experience​, with a focus on quantum computation. 
  • Firm understanding of systems programming, specifically low-level compiler details and linking techniques, with familiarity with the following: 
  • LLVM intermediate representation for classical instructions. 
  • ELF binary format at a high-level. 
  • QIR compiler infrastructure. 
  • Existing quantum intermediate representations including, but not limited to, OpenQASM 2 and 3 and QUIL.
  • Excellent interpersonal relationship building and coaching skills; able to communicate very clearly verbally and in written form. 

 

Desired Knowledge/Skills/Abilities  

  • Demonstrated ability to perform creative, high-quality academic work 
  • Track record of developing novel computational, statistical, or analytical tools​ 
  • Experience in business to business interaction, collaboratively identifying and solving interdisciplinary problems 
  • Experience programming in Python​, Rust, C++, or similar programming language. 
  • Experience using software development tools such as git
